
# PROBLEM STATEMENT

The rapid spread of fake news on social media and online platforms misleads public opinion and causes misinformation.

Detecting fake news automatically is challenging due to its evolving nature and subtle manipulation techniques.

There is a need for reliable, interpretable systems to assess the credibility of news content from diverse sources.

The project addresses the problem of identifying fake news articles by analyzing multiple credibility indicators and linguistic cues.

# SCOPE OF THE PROJECT

Focuses on textual news articles for binary classification: real vs. fake.

Incorporates source verification, emotional language, sensational keyword detection, grammar quality, and URL analysis.

Covers fact-checking claims against a knowledge base of known falsehoods.

Generates detailed credibility reports explaining the classification results.

Serves as a foundational tool that can be extended with multimedia analysis or real-time social media monitoring.

# TARGET USERS

News consumers seeking to verify the authenticity of online news.

Researchers and developers working on misinformation and fact-checking tools.

Social media platforms and news aggregators aiming to filter fake news.

Educational institutions promoting media literacy.

# HIGH-LEVEL FEATURES

Database of verified and unreliable news sources for cross-referencing.

Detection of sensational and emotional language markers in articles.

Credibility scoring system based on multiple factors including author, date, citations, and grammar.

Fact-checking mechanism against false claim repositories.

Suspicious URL pattern recognition.

Generation of detailed, user-friendly reports on news credibility.

Rule-based classification system enabling transparent and explainable decisions
